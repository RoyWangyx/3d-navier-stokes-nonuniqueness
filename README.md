# Rigorous Numerical Verification for Non-uniqueness of Leray–Hopf Solutions to Navier–Stokes Equation

## Overview

This repository contains a notebook-driven workflow for rigorous numerical verification using interval arithmetic. The computations focus on the self-similar solution and the associated eigenpair verification for the Navier–Stokes equation.

The numerical routines are implemented in Julia and imported directly into Jupyter notebooks to perform rigorous verification.

## Important Notes

- The Julia code under `src/` is NOT intended to be installed as a standalone package.
- It is a local numerical library used directly by the notebooks.
- All verification workflows are notebook-driven.

## Notebook Workflows (notebooks/)

The notebooks should be executed in the following order, as later notebooks depend on data and results generated by earlier ones:

### 1. get_bessel_zeros.ipynb
Computes and stores zeros of Bessel-related functions used in basis construction and operator assembly.

### 2. selfsimilar_profile_verification.ipynb
Verifies analytic bounds related to the self-similar profile, including residual norms and operator bounds involving the matrix function Q.

### 3. eigenpair_error_analysis.ipynb
Performs rigorous error analysis of approximate eigenpairs, including residual norms and supremum bounds.

### 4. finite_rank_construction.ipynb
Constructs finite-rank approximations of operators and verifies approximate eigenpairs through residual evaluation.

### 5. selfsimilar_linear_operator_finite_rank.ipynb
Verifies bounds and properties of the finite-rank approximation of the self-similar linear operator.

### 6. eigen_linear_operator_finite_rank.ipynb
Constructs and verifies solutions to the finite-rank eigenvalue problem, including residual bounds and regularity verification.

## Verified Quantities

The following quantities appearing in the paper are rigorously verified:

### selfsimilar_profile_verification.ipynb

Equation (4.44) - (4.46) in the paper:
- $L^2$ bounds of the residual $E_U$ for the self-similar profile $U$
- $W^{k,\infty}$ bounds of the matrix function $Q$
- $L^\infty$ bounds of the eigenvalues of $Q$
- verification of the positivity compensation condition:

  $Q + \frac{\nabla U + \nabla^T U}{2} + \eta I \ge 0$

---

### eigenpair_error_analysis.ipynb

Equation (4.47) - (4.51) in the paper:
- $L^2$ bounds of the residual $E_v$ for the eigenvector $v$
- $L^\infty$ bounds of the eigenvector $v$
- $L^\infty$ bounds of the gradient $\nabla v$
- verification of the normalization condition $\|v\|_{L^2} = 1$
- $L^2$ bounds of $\mathcal{L}^T v$, where $\mathcal{L}$ is the linearized operator

---

### finite_rank_construction.ipynb

Equation (4.52) - (4.55) in the paper:
- verification of matrix elements

  $A_{ij} = \langle \psi_i^{s}, Q \psi_j^{s} \rangle, \quad
  B_{ij} = \langle \psi_i^{s}, \psi_j^{s} \rangle_{H^1}$

  where $\psi_i^{s}$ are eigenfunctions of the vector Laplacian on the unit ball satisfying the boundary conditions described in the paper.

- verification of the finite-rank approximation errors $\varepsilon_3$ and $\varepsilon_4$, defined in Section 4.2 of the paper.

- verification of the approximate eigenpairs $(\lambda_i,\psi_i)$ of the eigenvalue problem

  $A \psi = \lambda_i B \psi$

  and verification of the spectral lower bound

  $\langle (A - \sum_{i=1}^k \lambda_i \psi_i \psi_i^T) u, u \rangle\ge \lambda_{k+1} \langle B u, u \rangle
  \quad
  \text{for all } u,$

  where $\lambda_i$, $\psi_i$, and $\lambda_{k+1}$ are approximate eigenvalues and eigenfunctions.

---

### selfsimilar_linear_operator_finite_rank.ipynb

Equation (4.56) - (4.60) in the paper:
- computation of the residual functions

  $r_i = \mathcal{L} \phi_i - Q \psi_i,$

  where $\mathcal{L}$ is the linearized operator, $\psi_i$ are the (approximate) eigenpairs of the generalized eigenvalue problem $A x = \lambda B x$ defined above, and $\phi_i$ are the constructed approximate solutions.

- verification of the inner product bounds

  $\langle G^R, G^{Q^2} \rangle, \quad
  \langle G^{Q^2}, G^{Q^2} \rangle$

  where we define the residual Gram matrix $G^R$ as

  $G^R_{ij} = \langle r_i, r_j \rangle,$

  and the Gram matrix $G^Q$ as

  $G^{Q^2}_{ij} = \langle Q \psi_i, Q \psi_j \rangle/\lambda_i\lambda_j,$

- verification of $L^2$ bounds of the interaction terms

  $\phi_i \cdot \nabla \phi_j$

  (with $\ell^2$ norm taken over the index $i,j$).

- verification of $L^\infty$ bounds of the gradients
  $\nabla \phi_i$
  and $L^2$ bounds of the functions $\phi_i$

  (with $\ell^2$ norm taken over the index $i$).

---

### eigen_linear_operator_finite_rank.ipynb
Equation (4.61) - (4.63) in the paper:
- computation of the residual functions $r_i$ similarly defined to those in
  `selfsimilar_linear_operator_finite_rank.ipynb`.

- verification of the same inner products involving $G^R$ and $G^Q$:
  $\langle G^R, G^Q \rangle, \quad \langle G^Q, G^Q \rangle,$
  where the matrices $G^R$ and $G^Q$ defined analogously to those in
  `selfsimilar_linear_operator_finite_rank.ipynb`

- $L^2$ bounds of the functions $\phi_i$ (with $\ell^2$ norm taken over the index $i$).

## Source Files (src/)

### NS_Numerics.jl
- Main module and entry point
- Sets dtype (Float64 or Interval{Float64})
- Loads dependencies and includes other source files
- Exports functions and types used in notebooks; the export lists also serve as a quick index to locate where a function or type is defined in the source files

### Matrix_Func.jl
- Composite Newton–Cotes quadrature utilities
- Provides rigorous integration error bounds
- Backbone for bound computations

### My_Fourier.jl
- Numeric helper functions tied to dtype
- Fourier and trigonometric utilities

### My_Scalar.jl
- Scalar function data structures
- Space and frequency representations
- Boundary condition handling

### My_Vector.jl
- Vector function container built from ScalarFuncs
- Supports vector-level operator and residual computations

### My_Operator.jl
- Defines Operator acting on scalar functions
- Supports matrix operators and multipliers

### My_Bessel.jl
- Bessel-function utilities and rigorous bounds
- Interval-based supremum bound computations

### Trig_Poly.jl
- Trigonometric polynomial implementation
- Supports multiplication, evaluation, and testing

### Comb_Bound.jl
- Computes W^{k,∞} derivative bounds
- Implements Leibniz and multinomial convolution bounds

### My_Assembly.jl
- Assembles analytic bounds and operator ingredients
- Connects special-function bounds and basis construction

### NS_Terms.jl
- Constructs operator terms
- Produces Operator objects used in notebooks
